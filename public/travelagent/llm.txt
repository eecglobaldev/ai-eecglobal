SECTION 3: FORENSIC FINANCIAL STANDARDS
================================================================================

Visa Officers reject applications primarily due to "Weak Economic Ties" or "Unverified Funds".

[3.1] ACCEPTABLE LIQUID FUNDS (TIER 1)
These are considered "Cash-Equivalent" and are highly trusted:
1.  **Savings Bank Account**: Statement must show 6 months history. Sudden large deposits are a RED FLAG.
2.  **Fixed Deposits (FD)**: Must be 3 months old or have a clear source.
3.  **Education Loan**: Sanction letter from a Nationalized Bank (SBI, BoB, etc.) is Gold Standard. NBFC loans (HDFC Credila, Avanse) require stricter co-applicant proofs.
4.  **Provident Fund (PF)**: Only the "Withdrawable Amount" counts. Need an official letter from the employer.

[3.2] UNACCEPTABLE / ILLIQUID FUNDS (TIER 3)
Do NOT use these for primary visa evidence:
1.  **Gold / Jewelry**: Valuation reports are secondary only. Not liquid.
2.  **Real Estate / Property**: Shows "Ties to Home" but DOES NOT count towards tuition/living fees.
3.  **Crypto-currency**: Completely unrecognized by consulates.
4.  **Stock Market/Mutual Funds**: Must be liquidated into a bank account to be counted.
5.  **Post Office Schemes**: Often rejected if not easily withdrawable.

[3.3] SPONSORSHIP HIERARCHY
1.  **Parents**: 100% Acceptance.
2.  **Grandparents**: High Acceptance (requires affidavit).
3.  **Siblings**: Moderate Acceptance (requires strong income proof).
4.  **Uncle/Aunt/Cousin**: LOW Acceptance (High Risk of Rejection). Avoid unless necessary.
5.  **Friends**: ZERO Acceptance. Never use non-blood relatives.

SECTION 4: REFUSAL CODES & ACADEMIC INTEGRITY
================================================================================

[4.1] USA: SECTION 214(b) - IMMIGRANT INTENT
- **Definition**: The officer assumes you intend to stay in the US permanently.
- **The Fix**: You must prove you have "Strong Ties" to India (Family, Property, Job Offer) that will compel you to return.
- **Forensic Check**: Does the DS-160 align with answers? Does the course make career sense in India?

[4.2] AUSTRALIA: GTE / GS (GENUINE STUDENT)
- **Gap Rule**: >2 years gap without solid work experience is a major rejection trigger.
- **Course Progression**: Moving from Master's back to Diploma? Automatic Rejection risk.
- **Financial Matrix**: Funds must be 3 months old. "Money in account yesterday" gets rejected.

[4.3] CANADA: SDS VS NON-SDS
- **SDS (Student Direct Stream)**: Requires IELTS 6.0 (each band), 1 Year Fees Paid, GIC ($20,635 CAD). High approval rate.
- **Non-SDS**: Any other profile. Slower processing, higher rejection risk.
- **Study Plan (SOP)**: The #1 reason for Canada refusal is a weak SOP. It must explain "Why Canada?" and "Why this course?".
